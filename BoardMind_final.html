Simpler Explanation — ANOVA

📘 Simplified from Lecture 7
ANOVA asks one question: "Are any of these groups really different, or is it just random luck?" Instead of comparing every pair one by one, ANOVA checks all groups at once. If the answer is yes → investigate which groups differ. If no → the differences are probably just noise. Think of it like testing 4 classrooms at once instead of running 6 separate comparisons.
